Format: GIF. Dimensions: 480w x 270h. Size: 221.7K. Settings: Selective, 128 Colours, Pattern Dither, 25 frames, Transparency on. No Transparency Dither. Non-Interlaced, 0% Web Snap. The GIF I have created would be intended to promote a children’s event where kids could meet Babbit and her friends in person at an organised activity day for children on a particular date, which I have prompted as August 21st for indication purposes for the assessment.

The text is limited and large enough for children to be able to read clearly and is legible with white text on a dark background. My Babbit event Gif is an indication of how such a promotion could be used through flashing headlines where appropriate and animation. I have used 25 frames each set at 0.2seconds per frame. It could be used on the Babbit website or on other commercial online promotions.

The size of the gif of 480w x 270h allows the image to be large enough for children to visualise properly on a website rather than a more traditional box size of 200x 200 alternative which would be too small for most children to notice. This hasn’t affected the Gif size, which is much smaller than the maximum recommended gif of 8MB coming in at only 221.K.

I used Pattern rather than diffusion because Pattern uses a square pattern of colour and simulates the missing colours. I found that diffusion selected a random pattern of colours and the gif created stripes across the screen. The increase in size was not significant, therefore I decided to use Pattern.

I chose a night scene as I felt it helped to focus more on Babbit and gave the flashing text and nose more emphasis. The Gif is intended to make the child feel that Babbit is travelling on her toy kitty by using animation that followed the road down and across the screen. The Gif ends with a large image of Babbit and her nose flashing with a small smile.
